INTERPRETATION:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
✓ CRITICAL FINDING: Inverted U-curve relationship!
  → Worst jams at INTERMEDIATE truck percentages (25%)
  → Better at both extremes (0% and 75%)

PHYSICAL MECHANISM:
  • At 0% trucks: Homogeneous fast traffic, minimal jams
  • At 25% trucks: MAXIMUM heterogeneity - cars constantly catching trucks
    - Speed differentials amplify stop-and-go oscillations
    - "Speed differential chaos"
  • At 50-75% trucks: Fleet becomes homogeneous again (mostly slow)
    - Lower heterogeneity = less oscillation amplification
    - "Slow but steady"

THESIS CONTRIBUTION:
  This demonstrates that HETEROGENEITY itself (speed variance) is more
  destabilizing than individual vehicle characteristics. Small minorities
  of either type create the worst conditions.

POLICY IMPLICATIONS:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
• COUNTERINTUITIVE: Small truck minorities are WORSE than large majorities
• Truck lane restrictions most valuable at 20-30% truck presence
• Consider "homogeneity-preserving" policies:
  - Separate lanes for different vehicle types
  - Time-of-day truck restrictions to avoid mixed conditions
  - Platooning to reduce effective heterogeneity
• Speed limits should account for heterogeneity, not just density
